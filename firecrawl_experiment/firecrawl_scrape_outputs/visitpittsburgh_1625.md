# Pittsburgh Celebrates Light Up Night 2025

Join us as we light up the city and officially kick-off the Pittsburgh holiday season!

[Highmark Light Up Night](http://downtownpittsburghholidays.com/) is returning **Saturday, Nov. 22, 2025** as a part of a larger 63rd Anniversary celebration. Annual festivities combine long-standing traditions and new holiday experiences with a supersized fireworks finale - an evening you won't want to miss!

## More Information for 2025 Coming Soon!

Please check back for information on this year's festivities! In the meantime you can explore the schedule of activities and events from our 2023 celebration.

The lighting of all the Three Sisters bridges, The Roberto Clemente, Rachel Carson and Andy Warhol, spanning [Downtown](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/neighborhoods/downtown-pittsburgh/) to the [North Shore](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/neighborhoods/north-shore-north-side/).

## Peoples Gas Holiday Market

In Market Square, you'll find the picturesque [Peoples Gas Holiday Market](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/blog/holiday-market/). Inspired by the original German Christkindlmarkts, the Holiday Market features national and local vendors housed in vintage, wooden chalets selling one-of-a-kind holiday gifts.

## Live Music

Spanning five stages and several genres of music, you'll find everything from national headliners to local legends to carolers. Find these and many other performances on pop-up stages throughout downtown and holiday carolers on stage at the Holiday Market. Follow the schedule below so you don't miss a thing.

## The Rink at PPG Place

Venture over to [PPG Place](http://www.ppgplace.com/) to ice skate around the beautifully decorated 66-foot illuminated Holiday tree on [The Rink at PPG Place](http://www.ppgplace.com/directory/the-rink/). The outdoor ice rink is open from the public starting November , 22 2025 through March 1, 2026, with special holiday hours for Thanksgiving, Christmas and New Year's (we highly recommend [reserving your tickets](http://www.ppgplace.com/directory/the-rink/) in advance).

## Fifth Avenue Place

[Fifth Avenue Place](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/directory/fifth-avenue-place/) is place to be on Light Up Night. The Highmark Christmas Tree Lighting will take place on the corner of Penn Ave. and Stanwix St., followed by two rooftop fireworks shows from Zambelli.

## Holiday KidsPlay & Selfie Garden

[Kids can have a blast](http://downtownpittsburghholidays.com/holiday-kidsplay) at Light Up Night, to! From noon-8 p.m. on Nov. 18, your little ones can do hands-on crafts, make a Mister Rogers sweater ornament, go on a learning adventure with Peg+Cat and more at Sixth St. and Liberty Ave. Then, head to the Heinz Hall Courtyard to venture through the colorful backdrops and standees featuring Daniel Tier and more Fred Rogers Productions' favorite characters from 2-6 p.m. on Light Up Night.

## Meet the Man in Red

What’s a holiday celebration without meeting the man of the hour, Santa Claus? Bring the kids and their wish lists because this year, they can visit with Santa in-person at his festive house in Heinz Hall Courtyard, thanks to BNY Mellon! Once again, families have the wonderful option for their young ones to visit with either a Black or White Santa. Just let Santa’s helpers know which you prefer!

## City-County Building

The beloved 21st annual Gingerbread house competition winners will be displayed in the lobby of the historic Pittsburgh City-County Building. Their 108th Official Tree, a 60-foot spruce from Lincoln Place with hundreds of ornaments as a tribute to the city's 90 neighborhoods, will be lit with festive colors at 5 p.m. followed by a rooftop fireworks display!

![A father lifts his child up to admire the Gingerbread house competition winners displayed at the Pittsburgh City-County Building](data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D%27http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg%27%20width%3D%271%27%20height%3D%271%27%20style%3D%27background%3Atransparent%27%2F%3E)

## Heinz Hall

Enjoy the sounds of the [Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/directory/pittsburgh-symphony-orchestra/) in the Cultural District for three free [Highmark Light Up Night Concerts](http://trustarts.org/event/26454-2023/highmark-light-up-night-r-concerts). No tickets are required, simply show up to [Heinz Hall](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/directory/heinz-hall/) at 6:30, 7:15 or 8 p.m. to enjoy the music.

## U.S. Steel Tower Plaza

See a larger-than-life nativity scene and the ONLY authorized replica of the nativity featured in Saint Peter’s Basilica in Rome. The Pittsburgh Crèche, which measures roughly 64 feet wide by 36 feet long and features 20 life-sized figures, has been an attraction since 1999.

## More Holiday Fun

There's so much to include during Light Up Night we can barely fit it into one blog. Some other highlights to check out Nov. 18 include:

- [Art Abound](http://downtownpittsburgh.com/emerging-visions-transforms-downtown-storefronts-into-unique-urban-gallery/) \- See the works of three Pittsburgh artists prominently displayed along Liberty Avenue as you pass by the storefronts.
- Pirates Mini-Fest - Take photos with the Pirate Parrot and your favorite racing pierogis along with kids activities and all things Bucco baseball from 3-9:30 p.m. on Stanwix St. and Ft. Duquesne Blvd.
- Highmark Holiday Block Party - Warm up and win prizes as you celebrate the holidays all night long on Penn Ave. and Stanwix St.
- Photos with Santa - Stanwix St. and Ft. Duquesne Blvd. at the Coca Cola Caravan.
- Food & Drinks - local vendors, food trucks and local beer can be found at the Riverside Stage and the corners of Stanwix and Penn, Grant and Fourth as well as Penn and 8th.
- [Holiday Pop-Ups](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/blog/pittsburgh-pop-up-bars/) \- [Miracle on 6th](http://www.instagram.com/miraclepgh/), decked floor to ceiling with Christmas kitsch and serving up festive mocktails and cocktails in amazing themed glassware, opens on Light Up Night. Also check out Barcadia for their Home Alone-themed experience.
- Kid-Friendly Fun - Hands-on crafts and activities 2-6 p.m. at 6th and Liberty. Plus, visit the Heinz Hall courtyard for photo ops with your kids' favorite characters and scenes from Fred Rogers Productions.
- Fifth Avenue Freeze - Master carver Rich Bubin will display his holiday carvings of The Grinch, Olaf, Elf and more at Fifth Avenue Place with additional activities inside.
- All Ages Film Festival - Stop by the Harris Theater for a free screening of family-friendly and festive films.
- Follow our [guide to downtown](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/blog/top-places-to-eat-in-downtown-pittsburgh/) for even more places to shop, eat and explore. Make reservations now!

## More Ways to Celebrate the Holidays

Looking for more festive activities? Embark on a shopping adventure across Pittsburgh and find the [perfect gifts](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/blog/pittsburgh-holiday-gift-guide/) for your loved ones. Be immersed in holiday light extravaganzas throughout the city, including Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens' [Holiday Magic! Winter Flower Show and Light Garden](http://www.phipps.conservatory.org/calendar/detail/holiday-magic), as well as [GLOWLAND](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/blog/glowland-pittsburgh-oakland/) in the [Oakland](https://www.visitpittsburgh.com/neighborhoods/oakland/) area.

Come celebrate unity, joy, and love the Pittsburgh way this holiday season!

Visit [downtownpittsburgh.com/holidays](http://downtownpittsburghholidays.com/) for the full line-up of events and the latest information.
